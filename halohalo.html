Halo-Halo

Halo-Halo, which is widely associated with Luzon, is a famous Filipino shaved ice dessert whose name means mixed-mix. It combines many sweet ingredients in one colorful and refreshing glass.

Popular mix-ins include sweetened saba bananas, jackfruit, beans, nata de coco, gulaman, and leche flan. Crushed ice and evaporated milk tie everything together.

It is often topped with ube halaya and ice cream, making it one of the most iconic treats for hot weather in the Philippines.

How to Cook

  1. Layer the Mix-ins:

    Place your chosen sweetened ingredients at the bottom of a tall glass.

  2. Add Ice and Milk:

    Fill the glass with crushed ice, then pour evaporated milk over the top.

  3. Top It:

    Add ube halaya, leche flan, and a scoop of ice cream.

  4. Serve:

    Serve immediately and mix well before eating to combine all flavors and textures.
