Tinola

Tinola is a traditional Filipino chicken soup made fragrant with ginger, garlic, and onions. It is a light but flavorful dish often cooked at home for everyday meals.

The soup usually includes green papaya or chayote and chili leaves or malunggay, giving it a fresh and nutritious character. Its clear broth makes it soothing and easy to pair with rice.

Because of its warm and gentle flavor, tinola is often served when someone is feeling under the weather or when families want a simple comforting meal.

How to Cook

  1. Saute Aromatics:

    Heat a little oil in a pot, then saute garlic, onion, and ginger until fragrant.

  2. Cook Chicken:

    Add chicken pieces and cook until lightly browned. Stir in fish sauce and cook for another minute.

  3. Simmer:

    Pour in water or broth, bring to a boil, then lower heat and simmer until chicken is tender.

  4. Add Vegetables:

    Add papaya or chayote and cook until tender. Add chili leaves or malunggay last and cook briefly.

  5. Serve:

    Season with pepper, then serve hot with steamed rice.
