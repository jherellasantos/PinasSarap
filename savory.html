OVERVIEW

Savory Filipino dishes are known for bold, comforting flavors built from garlic, vinegar, soy sauce, coconut milk, and local spices usually paired with rice. This category gathers rich and satisfying mains that are often served with rice and shared family-style. Each dish reflects regional identity while staying rooted in familiar Filipino taste profiles.

FILIPINO SAVORY DISHES

SISIG

Sisig is a popular Filipino dish made from chopped pork parts such as jowl, ears, and belly, usually seasoned with calamansi, onions, and chili peppers and served on a sizzling plate. It originated in Pampanga, particularly in Angeles City, and is considered a famous staple of Kapampangan cuisine.

BICOL EXPRESS

Bicol Express is a Filipino pork stew cooked in coconut milk, shrimp paste, and chili peppers. It has a creamy, savory, and spicy flavor, with a slight sweetness from the coconut milk that balances the heat. It is usually served with rice and enjoyed for its rich, bold taste.

IGADO

Igado is a savory Filipino dish from the Ilocos Region made with pork tenderloin and pig innards like liver and kidney. It is cooked in soy sauce and vinegar, giving it a rich, salty-sour flavor, and is often served with rice or as a dish for drinking sessions.

HUMBA

Sweet and savory braised pork belly with soy sauce, vinegar, brown sugar, and garlic. A comforting Negros specialty with rich caramelized flavors that pairs perfectly with steamed rice.

INASAL

Grilled chicken marinated in achuete, calamansi, garlic, ginger, and lemongrass. Famous from Bacolod, this smoky Visayan specialty is served with garlic rice and a tangy dipping sauce.

LAING

Laing is a Filipino dish made from dried taro leaves cooked in coconut milk. It is rich, creamy, and slightly spicy, often flavored with shrimp paste, garlic, ginger, onions, and chili peppers. The dish has a soft, tender texture and a savory taste, and it is commonly served with steamed rice.

CURACHA

Native to the deep waters of Zamboanga and Sulu, this unique red frog crab boasts a massive amount of sweet meat hidden within its main body. It reaches legendary status when smothered in a decadent, deeply spiced coconut and crab-fat sauce.

PIAPARAN A MANOK

A masterpiece of Maranao cuisine, this vibrant golden chicken stew is enriched with coconut milk, turmeric, and palapa. It is beautifully finished with heavily spiced, toasted grated coconut for a complex, earthy, and fiery flavor profile.
