Bicol Express

Although Bicol Express is closely associated with the Bicol region, it is widely believed to have been created in Manila by restaurateur Cely Kalaw in the 1960s. She developed the dish while experimenting with a milder, creamier version of laing, a traditional Bicolano dish. The name “Bicol Express” was inspired by the train route connecting Manila to Bicol, which she reportedly heard while thinking of a name. Despite this, many Bicolanos argue that the dish was inspired by their local recipe called ginataang sili (or gulay na lada), which uses similar ingredients such as coconut milk, pork, and shrimp paste. This suggests that while the name and popular version came from Manila, its roots are strongly influenced by Bicolano cuisine.

Bicol Express

How to Cook

  1. Combine pork, ginger, garlic, onion, Thai chili pepper, long green pepper, and bagoong alamang, coconut milk in a pan. Mix well. Cover the pan and turn the heat to on. Let the mixture boil.

  2. 2 lbs. pork belly, 2 cups coconut milk, 1/4 cup shrimp paste, 5 cloves garlic, 5 pieces Thai chili pepper, 2 thumbs ginger, 1 piece onion, 2 pieces Serrano pepper

  3. Stir and adjust the heat to low. Cover and simmer for 50 minutes. Note: add water as necessary

  4. Add the remaining coconut cream and bagoong alamang (as needed). Season with Maggi Magic Sarap. Continue cooking in low heat until the sauce thickens

  5. 2 cups coconut cream, 8 grams Maggi Magic Sarap

  6. Transfer to a serving plate and serve with warm rice.
