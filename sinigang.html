Sinigang na Baboy

Sinigang na Baboy, which is widely associated with Luzon, is one of the most iconic Filipino comfort soups, known for its sour and savory broth. It is typically made with pork, tomatoes, onions, and tamarind as the souring base.

Different regions and households use various souring agents like fresh sampalok, guava, kamias, or calamansi. The dish is often packed with vegetables, making it hearty and nourishing.

Served hot with rice and fish sauce with chili on the side, sinigang is especially loved during rainy weather and family meals.

How to Cook

  1. Simmer the Pork:

    In a pot, combine pork, water, onion, and tomatoes. Bring to a boil, skim scum, then simmer until pork is tender.

  2. Add Sour Base:

    Stir in tamarind soup mix (or fresh tamarind extract) and season with fish sauce.

  3. Cook Vegetables:

    Add radish and eggplant first, then string beans. Add kangkong and chilies last to keep them fresh and vibrant.

  4. Serve:

    Taste and adjust seasoning. Serve piping hot with steamed rice.
