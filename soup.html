OVERVIEW

Filipino soups are warm, aromatic, and deeply comforting. They often combine rich broths with meat or seafood and are flavored with local herbs, spices, and coconut-based ingredients. This category features hearty bowls commonly enjoyed during rainy days, family meals, and special gatherings.

FILIPINO SOUPS

LA PAZ BATCHOY

A noodle soup from Iloilo City made with egg noodles, pork offal, shrimp, and rich broth. Topped with chicharon, liver spread, and a raw egg, it is a beloved Visayan comfort food with centuries of culinary tradition.

BINAKOL

Chicken soup cooked in coconut water with ginger, lemongrass, and vegetables. A light and aromatic Visayan comfort food that captures the sweet essence of coconut in every spoonful.

BULALO

Bulalo, which originated in Luzon, is a hearty Filipino beef shank soup simmered for hours until the meat is tender and the marrow becomes rich and flavorful. It is commonly served hot with corn, cabbage, and potatoes.

SINIGANG NA BABOY

Sinigang na Baboy, which is widely associated with Luzon, is a classic Filipino sour pork soup simmered with tomatoes, tamarind broth, and fresh vegetables. Its comforting tangy flavor makes it a staple during rainy days and family meals.

TIYULA ITUM

A prestigious Tausug delicacy traditionally served at grand celebrations and weddings. This striking black soup gets its ink-dark color and unforgettable smoky, savory flavor from pamapa itum, a unique burnt coconut spice paste.
