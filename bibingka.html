Bibingka

Bibingka, which originated in Luzon, is a traditional Filipino rice cake often associated with Christmas mornings and Simbang Gabi. It is known for its soft, fluffy center and lightly charred edges.

Classic bibingka is baked in clay pots lined with banana leaves, which gives it a subtle smoky aroma. Modern home versions are usually baked in ovens but still keep the same comforting flavor profile.

It is commonly topped with salted egg, cheese, and grated coconut, creating a sweet and savory contrast that Filipinos love.

How to Cook

  1. Prepare the Pan:

    Preheat oven to 375 F (190 C). Line a round pan with banana leaf if available and lightly grease.

  2. Make the Batter:

    Whisk rice flour, baking powder, and sugar. Add eggs, coconut milk, and melted butter, then mix until smooth.

  3. Bake:

    Pour batter into the pan and bake for about 12 minutes. Remove briefly, add salted egg and cheese, then bake again for 8 to 10 minutes.

  4. Finish:

    Brush the top with butter and sprinkle grated coconut before serving warm.
