Turon

Turon, which is widely associated with Luzon street-food culture, is a sweet fried snack made with ripe saba bananas wrapped in thin lumpia wrappers.

Many versions add ripe langka (jackfruit) for extra aroma and flavor. When fried with sugar, the wrapper caramelizes into a crisp golden shell.

It is a popular merienda sold in homes, markets, and roadside stalls, usually served warm and crunchy.

How to Cook

  1. Prepare Filling:

    Roll each saba banana half in brown sugar and place jackfruit strips on top if using.

  2. Wrap:

    Place banana on a lumpia wrapper, fold both sides inward, and roll tightly. Seal edge with water.

  3. Fry:

    Heat oil over medium heat and fry turon until golden brown and crisp. Sprinkle a little extra sugar in the pan for caramelized coating.

  4. Serve:

    Drain excess oil and serve warm for best crunch.
