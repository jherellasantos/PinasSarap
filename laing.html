Laing

Laing originated in the Bicol region of the Philippines, where coconut milk is widely used in cooking. It is considered a traditional Bicolano dish and is deeply rooted in the region’s cuisine, which is known for its rich, spicy, and coconut-based flavors. Over time, laing became popular throughout the Philippines and even gained recognition internationally. Its simplicity and reliance on local ingredients like taro leaves and coconut milk reflect the agricultural lifestyle of the region, while its bold flavors highlight the distinctive culinary identity of Bicol.

Laing

How to Cook

  1. Combine the coconut milk, pork, shrimp paste, ginger, onion, and garlic in a cooking pot. Heat and bring to a boil.

  2. Once the mixture starts to boil, gently stir. Cover and simmer for 15 to 20 minutes. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.

  3. Add the dried taro leaves. Do not stir. Let the leaves absorb the coconut milk for 20 to 30 minutes. Gently push the leaves down if needed.

  4. Once the leaves have absorbed the liquid, stir and continue cooking for 10 minutes.

  5. Pour in the coconut cream and add the red chilies. Stir and cook for 10 to 12 minutes until the sauce thickens.

  6. Transfer to a serving plate. Serve hot and enjoy.
