Maruya

Maruya, which is commonly associated with the Visayas, is a Filipino banana fritter made by coating saba bananas in batter and frying them until crisp and golden.

Depending on the region, bananas may be sliced, mashed, or fanned out before frying, giving each version a distinct texture.

It is a classic merienda often dusted with sugar and served hot with coffee or tea.

How to Cook

  1. Prepare Bananas:

    Peel saba bananas and slice each lengthwise, or lightly flatten for fan-style maruya.

  2. Make Batter:

    In a bowl, whisk flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt. Add egg and milk, then mix into a smooth batter.

  3. Coat and Fry:

    Dip bananas into batter and fry in hot oil over medium heat until both sides are golden brown.

  4. Finish and Serve:

    Drain on paper towels and sprinkle with sugar while warm.
