Bulalo

Bulalo, which originated in Luzon, is a beloved Filipino beef marrow soup best known for its rich broth and tender beef shanks. It is simmered slowly until the meat softens and the marrow melts into the soup.

This comforting dish is popular in cool-weather places like Tagaytay and is often enjoyed as a family meal. Corn, cabbage, and potatoes add sweetness and texture to the hearty broth.

Bulalo is usually served piping hot with steamed rice and a side of fish sauce with calamansi and chili.

How to Cook

  1. Boil the Beef:

    In a large pot, combine beef shanks and water. Bring to a boil and skim off scum for a clear broth.

  2. Simmer:

    Add onion and peppercorns, then simmer on low heat for 2 to 3 hours until beef is fork-tender.

  3. Add Vegetables:

    Add corn and potatoes, then cook until tender. Add cabbage and pechay last so they stay crisp and bright.

  4. Season and Serve:

    Season with fish sauce and serve hot with rice.
