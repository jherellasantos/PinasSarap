OVERVIEW

Filipino snacks are flavorful, practical, and perfect for merienda or quick meals. They range from wrapped rice-based bites to grilled skewers and fresh seafood preparations. This category features dishes that are easy to enjoy on the go while still carrying strong regional character.

FILIPINO SNACKS

PASTIL

A beloved Maguindanaon comfort food, Pastil is the ultimate budget-friendly, on-the-go meal. It features steaming white rice topped with kagikit or finely shredded and spiced chicken, beef, or fish, all neatly wrapped in a fragrant banana leaf.

KINILAW

Raw seafood cured in vinegar and citrus, flavored with coconut milk, ginger, onions, and chili. A refreshing Visayan specialty often served as an appetizer or pulutan with a bright, tangy flavor profile.

SATTI

The ultimate Zamboangueno breakfast experience. Small skewers of charcoal-grilled meat are served completely submerged in a thick, sweet, and vividly spicy red-orange gravy, eaten alongside chunks of woven palm rice.

TURON

Turon is a popular Filipino snack made with ripe saba bananas and jackfruit wrapped in lumpia wrapper, then fried until golden and caramelized. It is sweet, crispy, and commonly sold as street food.

MARUYA

Maruya is a Filipino banana fritter made by coating sliced saba bananas in batter and frying them until crisp on the outside and soft inside. It is often sprinkled with sugar and enjoyed as merienda.
