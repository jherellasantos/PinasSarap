La Paz Batchoy

La Paz Batchoy is a noodle soup dish from Iloilo City that has become a beloved Visayan favorite. It is made with egg noodles, pork offal, shrimp, and chicken broth, creating a rich and savory flavor. The dish is typically topped with fried pork bits, chicharon, liver spread, and a raw egg that cooks in the hot broth.

Named after La Paz neighborhood in Iloilo, this iconic dish has been a staple since the 1930s and is often served at local shops and carenderias. The combination of tender noodles, savory broth, and crispy toppings makes Batchoy a comfort food that represents the culinary heritage of Iloilo. It is commonly enjoyed for breakfast, lunch, or even late-night meals, often accompanied by patis (fish sauce) for added flavor.

How to Cook

  1. Prepare the offal:

    Blanch the pork offal in boiling water for 3-5 minutes, then drain and set aside. This removes any impurities.


  2. Prepare the broth:

    Heat the chicken or pork broth in a large pot. Add minced garlic and sauté until fragrant. Add the blanched offal and shrimp, simmer until cooked.


  3. Cook the noodles:

    In a separate pot, boil water and cook the egg noodles until al dente. Drain and set aside.


  4. Assemble the dish:

    Place cooked noodles in a bowl, pour hot broth over them. Top with chicharon, liver spread, egg yolk, and green onions. Serve immediately with fish sauce on the side.
