Sisig

Sisig is a popular Filipino dish made from chopped pork parts such as jowl, ears, and belly, usually seasoned with calamansi, onions, and chili peppers and served on a sizzling plate. It originated in Pampanga, particularly in Angeles City, and is considered a famous staple of Kapampangan cuisine.

The modern sizzling sisig became popular in Angeles City, Pampanga. It began along Glaciano Valdez, near the "Crossing" of the old train station in the 1970s where Lucia Cunanan (Aling Lucing), who owned a small hawker stand, started chopping pork ears, jowls, and maskara after accidentally overcooking them and soaking them in vinegar. She served the dish as pulutan (bar food) for beer drinkers, including locals and American servicemen stationed at Clark Air Base. To this day, sizzling sisig is commonly served in bars and restaurants, where it has become highly popular and in demand. It has even been adapted into instant versions, allowing it to be enjoyed anytime and anywhere.

How to Cook

  1. Make sisig sauce:

    Add oil to a skillet set over high heat. When the pan starts smoking, add chicken liver. Season with salt and fry until a crust forms on one side, about 2 minutes. Reduce heat to medium-low. Flip liver and fry until cooked through. Transfer to a medium bowl.

    Mash liver with a fork. Add mayonnaise and liquid seasoning. Mix until liver is well-incorporated. Set aside until ready to use.

  2. Prepare pork belly:

    Pour water in a deep pot set over high heat. Bring to a boil. Season with salt and vinegar, stirring to combine. Add pork belly. Cook until fat turns translucent and soft, about 10-15 minutes. Don't over-boil; the skin won't crisp up if too much water is absorbed.

    Using tongs, transfer pork belly to a wire rack or paper towel-lined plate. Allow pork to dry for 10 minutes. Pat off any excess moisture with paper towels.

  3. Fry and assemble:

    Heat oil in a sizzling plate or skillet set over high heat until smoking. Add pork belly and fry until edges are crispy and browned, about 5-7 minutes. Add chopped chili, minced onion, and crushed chicharon. Toss to combine.

    Pour in the sisig sauce and stir until everything is well coated. Add hot sauce and liquid seasoning to taste. Serve hot on the sizzling plate with steamed rice, fried egg, and calamansi wedges on the side.
