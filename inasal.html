Inasal

Chicken Inasal is a Visayan grilled chicken dish marinated in achuete, calamansi, garlic, ginger, and lemongrass. It is especially linked to Bacolod and is celebrated for its smoky aroma and bright, tangy glaze.

The name "inasal" means "to grill" in Filipino, and the dish is traditionally served with garlic rice and a dipping sauce of vinegar, soy, and chili. Its distinctive marinade makes it one of the most beloved grilled specialties from the Visayas.

How to Cook

  1. Make the marinade:

    Mix calamansi juice, minced garlic, grated ginger, annatto oil, soy sauce, brown sugar, and salt in a bowl.


  2. Marinate the chicken:

    Place the chicken pieces in a shallow dish. Add the marinade and lemongrass. Turn to coat and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight.


  3. Grill the chicken:

    Preheat the grill to medium-high. Grill the chicken skin-side down first, brushing with reserved marinade until cooked through and slightly charred.


  4. Serve warm:

    Enjoy with steamed rice, pickled vegetables, and extra calamansi for squeezing.
