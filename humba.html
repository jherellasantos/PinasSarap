Humba

Humba is a sweet and savory braised pork dish from the Visayas, especially popular in Negros. It features tender pork belly, soy sauce, vinegar, brown sugar, and garlic, often with banana blossoms or black beans.

The slow-cooked flavors in humba create a rich caramelized sauce that pairs perfectly with steamed rice. This hearty comfort food is a staple at family meals and fiestas across the region.

How to Cook

  1. Brown the pork:

    In a pot, sauté garlic until fragrant. Add the pork belly and cook until lightly browned.


  2. Add seasonings:

    Pour in soy sauce, vinegar, brown sugar, peppercorns, and bay leaves. Stir to combine.


  3. Simmer until tender:

    Add water to cover the pork. Cover and simmer over low heat for 1 to 1.5 hours until the pork is tender and the sauce is thickened.


  4. Finish and serve:

    Stir in banana blossoms or pineapple during the last 10 minutes. Serve humba with hot rice.
