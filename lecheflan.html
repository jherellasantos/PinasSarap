Leche Flan

Leche Flan, which is closely associated with Luzon, is a classic Filipino custard dessert known for its silky texture and rich caramel topping. It is a staple during fiestas, holidays, and family celebrations.

The dish traces influences from Spanish flan but developed into a richer local version by using more egg yolks and condensed milk. The result is dense, creamy, and deeply satisfying.

It is usually chilled and served in slices, often as a stand-alone dessert or as a topping for halo-halo.

How to Cook

  1. Make the Caramel:

    Heat sugar in each mold over low heat until melted and golden. Swirl to coat the bottom evenly, then let it cool.

  2. Mix the Custard:

    In a bowl, gently whisk egg yolks, condensed milk, evaporated milk, and vanilla until smooth. Avoid overmixing to prevent bubbles.

  3. Steam:

    Pour the mixture into molds, cover with foil, and steam over low heat for 30 to 40 minutes or until set.

  4. Chill and Serve:

    Cool completely, refrigerate, then unmold by running a knife around the edge and flipping onto a plate.
