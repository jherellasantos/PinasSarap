Ube Halaya

Ube Halaya, which is associated with the Visayas, is one of the most beloved Filipino desserts made from purple yam slowly cooked with milk, sugar, and butter. Its rich texture and vibrant color make it a standout centerpiece during celebrations.

Traditionally prepared by hand over low heat, the dish requires constant stirring until the mixture becomes smooth and glossy. This patient method gives ube halaya its signature creamy consistency.

Families often serve it during holidays, fiestas, and birthdays, either on its own or as a base for other sweets like cakes, pastries, and halo-halo. It is both nostalgic and festive in Filipino food culture.

How to Cook

  1. Prepare the Yam:

    Boil the ube until tender, peel, then grate or mash until smooth.

  2. Cook the Mixture:

    In a pan over low heat, combine grated ube, condensed milk, evaporated milk, sugar, and butter. Stir continuously to prevent burning.

  3. Thicken:

    Keep stirring for 25 to 35 minutes until the mixture becomes thick, glossy, and pulls away from the sides of the pan.

  4. Set and Serve:

    Transfer to a greased container, smooth the top, and cool. Add latik or cheese before serving if desired.
